O-Ring Size Chart

AS568 and metric o-ring size reference for rubber and FFKM seals.

Using This Size Chart

Start by identifying whether your housing follows AS568 (inch) or ISO 3601 (metric) standards. Measure the groove ID/OD and width with calibrated tools, then use the tables below to locate the corresponding dash number or metric reference.

Always verify that the cross-section squeeze and stretch remain within the recommended range for the chosen elastomer. Garlast engineers can review your hardware drawings to confirm fit, compression set performance, and long-term reliability.

Measurement Tips

  • Measure O-rings without stretch at 20°C (68°F) using optical comparators or mandrels.
  • Confirm groove tolerances and surface finish to avoid pinch or spiral failure.
  • Apply appropriate stretch (up to 5% for large IDs, less for small parts) and squeeze (15-30%).
  • Account for thermal expansion and media swell when sizing for high-temperature applications.

Installation & Fit Guidelines

Static Face Seals

Target 15–25% squeeze on the cross-section. Ensure groove depth accounts for thermal expansion and media swell to avoid over-compression.

Radial (Piston/rod) Seals

Limit stretch to 2% for piston applications and keep inside diameter stretch under 5% for rod seals to preserve sealing force without overstressing the O-ring.

Dynamic Applications

Use low-friction coatings or lubricants, and verify groove edge radii to minimize abrasion. Consider back-up rings for high differential pressure.

Quality Assurance

Inspect incoming O-rings for surface defects, flat spots, and lot traceability. Store in sealed bags away from UV, ozone, and extreme temperatures.
